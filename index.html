Research Interests

Coastal community ecology, foundation species and large-scale patterns in marine ecosystems.

Coastal community ecology Foundation species Seagrasses Macroalgae Rhodoliths Macroecology Biogeography Ecosystem functioning Conservation

My research is motivated by the necessity of pursuing models that explain patterns of organization and functioning of marine biota across scales of space, time and taxonomy, with a particular interest on nearshore habitats underpinned by foundation species, particularly seagrasses and macroalgae (e.g. brown seaweeds), and, more recently, rhodoliths.

I aim to connect responses from physiological, population and ecosystem levels, with a strong focus on ecological patterns and processes at large scales. This has led to a growing emphasis on marine macroecology and biogeography, with the goal of transferring ecological knowledge to the sustainable management and conservation of marine ecosystems.
